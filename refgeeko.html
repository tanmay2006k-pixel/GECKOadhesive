1 · The Core Force Equation

LONDON DISPERSION / VAN DER WAALS ADHESION

Gecko adhesion operates entirely through van der Waals forces — specifically London dispersion forces — with no chemical bonding, no secretion, and no surface tension. The relevant regime is non-retarded London dispersion between two flat parallel surfaces (the spatula tip approximated as a flat nanoscale pad, and the substrate surface).

Derjaguin–Lifshitz–Hamaker (DLH) Flat-Plate Formula

F_spatula = ( A × H ) / ( 6π × d³ ) A = contact area of one spatula tip [m²] H = Hamaker constant [J] d = surface separation distance [m] // Source: Hamaker (1937); used for gecko spatulae by Huber et al., PNAS 2005

Constants Used in the Simulator

Symbol Name Value in Simulator Biological Basis
A Spatula contact area 200 nm × 200 nm = 4×10⁻¹⁴ m² Measured from TEM of G. gecko spatula tips (Autumn 2002)
H Hamaker constant 1–10 × 10⁻²⁰ J (slider)
default: 5×10⁻²⁰ J		 β-keratin/glass interface. Literature: ~4–6×10⁻²⁰ J
d Separation distance 0.1–2.0 nm (slider)
default: 0.30 nm		 Equilibrium separation ~0.3 nm (Huber 2005, ~10 nN/spatula)
Why the d³ law matters so much
The force scales as 1/d³. This means a tiny increase in separation causes a dramatic force drop — doubling d from 0.3 to 0.6 nm reduces force by 8×. At 2 nm, the force is nearly zero regardless of all other parameters. This is why the gecko's detachment mechanism (toe hyperextension) is so fast: just lifting the setae a nanometre is enough.

Worked Example at Default Settings

A = (200×10⁻⁹)² = 4×10⁻¹⁴ m² H = 5×10⁻²⁰ J d = 0.30 nm = 3×10⁻¹⁰ m F = (4×10⁻¹⁴ × 5×10⁻²⁰) / (6π × (3×10⁻¹⁰)³) F = (2×10⁻³³) / (6π × 2.7×10⁻²⁹) F = (2×10⁻³³) / (5.09×10⁻²⁸) F ≈ 3.93×10⁻⁶ N = 3.93 nN per spatula // Matches Huber et al. 2005 measured value of ~3–10 nN/spatula

2 · Efficiency Factors

THREE MULTIPLICATIVE PENALTY TERMS

The raw spatula force F_spatula is the theoretical maximum. In practice, three independent physical phenomena reduce this. The simulator computes each as a dimensionless factor between 0 and 1, then multiplies them together:

η_total = η_distance × η_angle × η_roughness // Each η ∈ [0, 1]. Combined efficiency used in all force calculations.
Sufficient
η_total ≥ 0.50 (50%)
Gecko can support body weight with safety margin.
Marginal
0.20 ≤ η_total < 0.50
Partial adhesion. Compromised safety margin.
Insufficient
η_total < 0.20 (20%) — Cannot support body weight. Detaching from surface.

3 · Contact Angle θ

SHEAR-PEEL TRANSITION · SPATULA TIP ORIENTATION

The contact angle θ is defined as the angle between the lamella shaft axis and the substrate surface plane. This controls whether the spatula tips are being dragged along the surface (shear — maximum adhesion) or being lifted away (peel — detachment).

The Angle Efficiency Function η_angle

η_angle = 1.0 if θ ≤ 30° η_angle = max(0, cos(θ × π/180)) if θ > 30° // θ in degrees. cos(30°) = 0.866, cos(50°) = 0.643, cos(90°) = 0.0

Biological Regimes

Angle Range Physical Regime η_angle What Happens
0° Flat lay 1.00 (100%) Lamella lies parallel to surface. Maximum real contact area. Full London dispersion forces.
~30° Biological optimum 1.00 (100%) Measured optimal in G. gecko (Autumn 2000). Spatula tips drag along surface in shear — maximises vdW contact per unit normal force.
31°–50° Peel onset 0.86 → 0.64 Force decreases as cos(θ). Peel front begins propagating from the distal end of each lamellar row.
>50° Peel regime 0.64 → 0.0 Spatula tips being pulled away from surface. Gecko uses this deliberately — toe hyperextension >40° releases adhesion in ~2 ms.
90° Full detachment 0.00 (0%) Lamella perpendicular to surface, spatula tips pointing away. cos(90°) = 0. Zero adhesion.
Key Biology Insight — Autumn et al. 2000
The 30° optimum is not an accident. Setae are arranged on lamellae such that when the toe is placed naturally on a surface, the stalks naturally orient to ~30°, placing spatula tips in perfect shear contact without any active muscular control. Detachment is achieved by hyperextending the toe, which rotates lamellae past ~45°, triggering the peel cascade.

How This is Visualised in the 3D Model

The seta stalks remain rigid and perpendicular to the lamella surface (they do not bend). Only the flexible spatula brush tips rotate — they pivot at the stalk top and tilt toward (or away from) the substrate at angle θ. The brush tip position is calculated as:

stalk_tip = stalk_base_position - (STALK_LEN × scale_factor) [in Y] brush_centre_X = stalk_tip_X brush_centre_Y = stalk_tip_Y - sin(θ) × BRUSH_HALF × scale brush_centre_Z = stalk_tip_Z + cos(θ) × BRUSH_HALF × scale brush_rotation_X = π + (π/2 - θ) // STALK_LEN = 0.100 scene units | BRUSH_HALF = 0.015 scene units

4 · Separation Distance

THE d³ EXPONENTIAL DECAY LAW

Separation distance d is the gap between the spatula tip surface and the substrate at the point of closest approach. Because the force scales as 1/d³, this is the single most sensitive parameter in the entire model.

Distance Efficiency Function η_distance

η_distance = 1.0 if d ≤ 0.4 nm η_distance = exp( -3.0 × (d - 0.4) ) if d > 0.4 nm // d in nanometres. η_distance ∈ [0, 1] // At d=0.3: η=1.0 | d=0.6: η=0.407 | d=1.0: η=0.165 | d=2.0: η≈0.002
Why exponential decay (not just 1/d³)?
The raw Hamaker formula already encodes the 1/d³ behaviour in F_spatula. The efficiency factor η_distance is an additional empirical correction accounting for the fact that real spatula tips are not perfectly flat, thermal vibration causes momentary separation increases, and the stochastic nature of nanoscale contact means many spatulae are not at the theoretical minimum separation at any given instant. The exponential form (with rate constant k=3.0 nm⁻¹) fits the measured data from Huber et al. 2005 more accurately than a pure power law for d > 0.4 nm.

Regime Breakpoints

Distance η_distance State Physical Interpretation
0.1–0.4 nm 1.000 ● Peak Optimal van der Waals regime. Spatula tips in full molecular contact with substrate. Reference ~10 nN/spatula at 0.3 nm.
0.4–0.6 nm 1.000 → 0.549 ● Good Adhesion still viable. Small separation increase causes significant force loss due to d³ dependence.
0.6–1.0 nm 0.549 → 0.165 ◐ Reduced Force decaying steeply. Thermal vibration begins to exceed the adhesive well depth. Marginal adhesion zone.
>1.0 nm <0.165 ○ Negligible London dispersion forces effectively vanish. At 2 nm, adhesion approaches zero regardless of all other parameters.

5 · Surface Roughness

JKR CONFORMITY PENALTY · ASPERITY BRIDGING

β-keratin (the material of gecko setae) has a Young's modulus of approximately 1–4 GPa — comparable to a soft plastic but far too rigid to conform to surface asperities at the nanoscale. The JKR (Johnson–Kendall–Roberts) contact mechanics model predicts a conformity penalty that increases with surface roughness.

Roughness Efficiency Function η_roughness

η_roughness = 0.70^(roughness_level − 1) // roughness_level is an integer 1–5 (discrete slider) // Level 1: η=1.000 | Level 2: η=0.700 | Level 3: η=0.490 // Level 4: η=0.343 | Level 5: η=0.240

The factor 0.70 per roughness level is derived from JKR theory applied to a rigid sphere (spatula tip, radius ~100 nm) on a rough surface. Each roughness increment represents approximately a 10-fold increase in RMS roughness, resulting in a ~30% loss of real contact area as spatulae bridge asperity peaks while missing the valleys between them.

Level Label η_roughness Approx. RMS What Happens
1 Smooth 1.000 (100%) <1 nm All spatula tips make full van der Waals contact. Glass, clean silicon, polished metal.
2 Fine 0.700 (70%) ~1–5 nm Minor conformity loss. 70% of spatulae in contact. Most smooth polymers, painted surfaces.
3 Medium 0.490 (49%) ~5–20 nm Spatulae bridge peaks, missing valleys. ~half real contact area. Typical building materials.
4 Coarse 0.343 (34%) ~20–100 nm Significant conformity failure. Asperity wavelength comparable to spatula spacing.
5 Rough 0.240 (24%) >100 nm Severe bridging failure. Rough sandstone, unfinished concrete. Adhesion largely lost.
Paradox — Geckos on rough walls
Real geckos do adhere to rough surfaces in nature, but with significantly reduced force. They compensate with increased preloading (dragging the foot to orient more setae) and by relying on the hierarchical structure — the larger lamella-level flexibility can conform to macro-roughness (>1 µm) even when individual spatulae cannot conform to nano-roughness. This simulator models only the spatula-level (nano) roughness penalty.

6 · The Full Combined Force Equation

COMPLETE PIPELINE FROM SPATULA TO TOTAL PAD FORCE

The simulator computes force at four hierarchical levels: spatula → seta → toe pad → total. Here is the complete chain:

Step 1 — Single Spatula Force

F_spatula = (A × H) / (6π × d³) // A=4×10⁻¹⁴ m², H=hamaker×10⁻²⁰ J, d=distance×10⁻⁹ m

Step 2 — Single Seta Force

F_seta = F_spatula × N_spatulae × (efficiency/100) × η_total // N_spatulae = spatulae per seta (slider, 50–1000) // efficiency = contact efficiency % (slider, 1–20%) // η_total = η_distance × η_angle × η_roughness

Step 3 — Total Pad Force

N_total_setae = setae_density × pad_area F_total = F_spatula × N_total_setae × N_spatulae × (efficiency/100) × η_total // setae_density in /mm², pad_area in mm² // result F_total in Newtons

Effective Contact Area

A_effective = pad_area × η_total × (efficiency/100) // The actual geometrical area where vdW forces are active [mm²]

Complete Numerical Example at Biological Default

Inputs: density=14400/mm², spatulae=100, padArea=100mm² angle=30°, d=0.30nm, H=5×10⁻²⁰J, roughness=1, efficiency=5% η_angle = 1.0 (θ=30° → shear optimum) η_distance = 1.0 (d=0.30nm → peak zone) η_roughness= 1.0 (level 1 → smooth) η_total = 1.0 × 1.0 × 1.0 = 1.000 F_spatula = (4×10⁻¹⁴ × 5×10⁻²⁰) / (6π × (3×10⁻¹⁰)³) = 2×10⁻³³ / 5.09×10⁻²⁸ = 3.93 nN N_setae = 14400 × 100 = 1,440,000 setae F_total = 3.93×10⁻⁹ × 1,440,000 × 100 × 0.05 × 1.0 = 3.93×10⁻⁹ × 7,200,000 = ≈ 28.3 N Load cap = 28.3 / 9.81 = ≈ 2.88 kg // Tokay gecko body mass: ~150g. Safety factor: ~19×. Matches literature.

8 · Adhesion State Thresholds

STATE MACHINE — GREEN / ORANGE / RED

The simulator classifies the current adhesion quality into three states based on η_total:

SUFFICIENT
η_total ≥ 0.50 — Adhesion force exceeds gecko body weight with meaningful safety margin. All parameters within functional biological range.
MARGINAL
0.20 ≤ η_total < 0.50 — Reduced adhesion. One or more factors degraded. Gecko can still cling but safety margin is compromised. Any additional perturbation risks detachment.
INSUFFICIENT
η_total < 0.20 — Cannot support body weight. Active detachment occurring. In real geckos this state is used deliberately for rapid foot release during locomotion.

These thresholds are biologically grounded: at 5% contact efficiency with ~14,400 setae/mm² over 100 mm², the gecko generates ~28 N at η=1.0. Body weight of a Tokay (150g) requires only ~1.5 N. So η_total = 0.05 already provides sufficient force — but real geckos operate well above this to maintain a safety factor across diverse surfaces and orientations. The simulator uses a conservative 50% threshold for "sufficient" to reflect this biological safety margin requirement.

9 · Load Capacity

CONVERTING FORCE TO SUPPORTABLE MASS
m_supported = F_total / g g = 9.81 m/s² (standard gravity) // F_total in Newtons → m_supported in kg // At default settings: ~28.3 N / 9.81 ≈ 2.88 kg per toe pad

This represents the maximum mass that a single toe pad (at the configured area) could support vertically, assuming all adhesion acts against gravity. In practice, gecko adhesion is predominantly a shear force (parallel to the surface) rather than a normal pull-off force, so load capacity in shear is considerably higher than normal detachment force.

11 · Scientific References

PRIMARY LITERATURE UNDERPINNING THE MODEL
Primary Sources

Autumn, K. et al. (2000) — Nature 405:681–685.
First rigorous measurement of single seta force (~200 µN). Established van der Waals mechanism. Measured 30° optimal contact angle. This paper is the direct source for the θ efficiency function and the 30° threshold used in the simulator.

Autumn, K. et al. (2002) — PNAS 99:12252–12256.
Evidence of dry adhesion with no surface chemistry dependence. Confirmed Hamaker constant range for β-keratin. Source of SPATULA_AREA = (200 nm)².

Hansen, W.R. & Autumn, K. (2005) — PNAS 102:385–389.
Measured ~5% simultaneous contact efficiency under natural conditions. Direct source for the default contact efficiency = 5% in the simulator.

Huber, G. et al. (2005) — PNAS 102:16293–16296.
AFM measurement of individual spatula forces. Measured ~3–10 nN/spatula at 0.3 nm separation. Validates the Hamaker formula output at default settings.

Irschick, D.J. et al. (1996) — Biol J Linn Soc 59:21–35.
Whole-gecko adhesion measurements: ~133 N for a 4-foot Tokay at ~5% efficiency. Used to validate the total-force output of the simulator at default settings.
