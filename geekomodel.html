About This Model
Tokay Gecko (Gekko gecko) — two-scale anatomy.

Contact angle θ = angle between individual seta shaft and the substrate surface. 0° = seta lies flat along surface (maximum shear contact area). 30° = biological optimum. 90° = seta perpendicular to surface.

Visual: Only the setae on each toe pad tilt — fingers/toes stay fixed. This is accurate: in real geckos, setae are dragged along the surface and the macroscopic toe geometry changes very little.

Mechanism: Pure London dispersion (van der Waals) forces. No glue, no suction. Self-cleans via contact mechanics.
Parameter Science
Setae Density 5k–20k /mm²
Tokay: ~14,400/mm² (Autumn 2002). Scales total force linearly. Physical ceiling ~20k from elastic array compliance. Green >10k, orange 7–10k, red <7k.
Spatulae per Seta 50–1000
Tokay avg ~100–1000 spatulae/seta (Autumn 2002). Each acts independently — statistical averaging gives stable adhesion. More spatulae = higher seta force.
Lamellae Count 10–25 ridges
Ventral toe-pad ridges. Tokay: 18 per toe. Orient setae perpendicular to pull direction — maximises shear adhesion and distributes load.
Toe Pad Area 50–200 mm²
Scales seta count proportionally. Full 4-foot contact: ~2000 mm². Theoretical max ~1440 N, measured ~133 N at 5% contact efficiency (Irschick 1996).
Contact Angle θ (seta–surface) 0–90°
The angle between the seta shaft and the substrate. 0° = seta flat on surface (max contact area). ~30° = biological optimum (Autumn 2000) — best balance of contact area and directionality. Above 30°: peel onset. Above 50°: rapid detachment.
Optimum: 15–30° | Threshold: 30° | Peel: >50° (Autumn et al. 2000)
Separation Distance 0.1–2.0 nm
van der Waals F ∝ 1/d³. Peak at 0.2–0.4 nm (~10 nN/spatula, Huber 2005). Beyond 0.6 nm: exponential decay. At 2 nm: effectively zero. Green <0.4 nm, orange 0.4–0.7 nm, red >0.7 nm.
Optimal: 0.2–0.4 nm | Cutoff: ~0.6 nm (Huber et al. 2005)
Hamaker Constant 1–10 ×10⁻²⁰ J
β-keratin / glass: H ≈ 5×10⁻²⁰ J (Autumn 2002). Material property — force multiplier. Does not affect efficiency factors. Higher = stronger inherent vdW interaction.
Surface Roughness Smooth→Rough
Each roughness step ×0.70 penalty (JKR contact mechanics). Rigid β-keratin spatulae cannot conform to asperities — reducing real contact area. Smooth glass: full adhesion. Sandpaper: near zero. Green lvl 1, orange lvl 2–3, red lvl 4–5.
JKR model: ×0.70 per roughness step
Contact Efficiency 1–20%
Only ~5% of spatulae make simultaneous vdW contact (Hansen & Autumn 2005). Accounts for spatial misalignment, elastic preload, surface wave propagation, and thermal vibration.
