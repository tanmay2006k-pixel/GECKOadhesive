About This Model
Contact angle θ = angle between the lamella shaft and the substrate surface.

0° — lamellae lies flat along surface. Maximum contact area, maximum vdW force.
~30° — biological optimum (Autumn 2000). Best shear contact.
>30° — peel onset. Force drops as cos(θ).
>50° — peel regime. Detachment.

Visual: Seta stalks always point perpendicular to the pad (they are rigid). Only the flexible spatula brush tips tilt — they pivot at the stalk top and lean toward the surface at angle θ. Toes and fingers never move.
Parameter Science
Setae Density 5k–20k /mm²
Tokay: ~14,400/mm². Scales total force linearly. Green >10k, orange 7–10k, red <7k.
Spatulae per Seta 50–1000
Tokay avg ~100–1000. Each acts independently. More spatulae = higher seta force.
Lamellae Count 10–25 ridges
Tokay: 18/toe. Ridges orient setae perpendicular to pull — maximises shear adhesion.
Toe Pad Area 50–200 mm²
Full 4-foot: ~2000 mm². Measured whole-gecko: ~133 N at 5% contact eff. (Irschick 1996).
Contact Angle θ 0–90°
Spatula tip angle from surface. 0°=flat max contact. 30°=bio optimum. >30° peel onset. >50° detachment. Visualised as the spatula brush cone tilt on each pad.
Critical: 30° | Peel: >50° (Autumn 2000)
Separation Distance 0.1–2.0 nm
F∝1/d³. Peak at 0.2–0.4 nm (~10 nN/spatula). Beyond 0.6 nm: exponential decay. At 2 nm: negligible.
Optimal: 0.2–0.4 nm (Huber 2005)
Hamaker Constant 1–10 ×10⁻²⁰ J
β-keratin/glass: H≈5×10⁻²⁰ J. Acts as force multiplier only — does not affect efficiency factors.
Surface Roughness Smooth→Rough
JKR ×0.70 penalty per step. Rigid β-keratin cannot conform to asperities. Green lvl 1, orange lvl 2–3, red lvl 4–5.
Contact Efficiency 1–20%
~5% simultaneous spatula contact (Hansen & Autumn 2005). Misalignment + elastic preload + thermal vibration.
